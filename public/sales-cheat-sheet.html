Local Business Website Sales Cheat Sheet
$100 setup  ·  $50/mo Cold Call Quick Reference
01
Quick Pitch — When They Answer

You've already built their site. You're not pitching a concept — you're handing them something real.

▸ The standard opener
"Hey, is this [name]? My name's [your name] — so this is a bit of an unusual call. I put together a starter website for [Business Name] and wanted to show it to you before doing anything else with it. Fair warning — it's a template right now, I didn't have much to go on. But it gives you a real sense of what it could look like once we fill it in with your actual info. You got two minutes to take a look?"
▸ If they sound skeptical or guarded
"I'll be straight with you — it's a starting point, not a finished product. I built it from your Google listing since that's all I had. But the structure is there, it's live, and if you like the direction we'd update everything — your photos, your hours, whatever you want. Can I just text you the link?"
▸ If they sound rushed
"Real quick — I put a starter site together for your business, it's live right now. It's generic at the moment but I can customize it once you're on board. Can I text you the link to take a look when you get a minute?"

SET THE EXPECTATION EARLY — IT HELPS YOU:

Telling them upfront it's a template means they won't feel misled when they see it. It also naturally opens the door to the real pitch: "imagine this with your actual photos, your real services, exactly how you want it." That's what they're buying — not the template, but the finished version of it.

02
Key Benefits — Lead With These
🔍
Show up when people search Without a site, you're invisible on Google. A website makes you findable 24/7 by people actively looking for what you offer right now.
✅
Look legit — instantly People Google businesses before they call. If there's nothing to find, they move on to a competitor who has a site. A website tells people: "We're real, we're professional, we're trustworthy."
⭐
Make your reviews visible Your happy customers already left reviews somewhere. A website brings that social proof front and center so new customers see it the moment they land on your page.
📞
Capture calls & leads you're missing Word-of-mouth still drives business — but people verify before they call. A site catches the referrals that would otherwise Google you, find nothing, and call someone else.
🏆
Beat local competitors If your competitor has a site and you don't, they're getting the clicks. A basic, clean website levels the playing field immediately.
05
The Close — Keep It Simple

They've seen the template. Now sell the finished version — that's what the $100 is for.

"So what you're looking at right now is just the starting point — the layout, the structure, the bones. For $100 we take that and turn it into something that actually looks like your business. Your photos, your services written the way you'd want them, your contact info, all of it. Then it's $50 a month and we keep it live, maintained, and updated whenever you need something changed. Want to move forward?"

If they want to see more before committing:

"Tell me one thing you'd want on it that isn't there right now. [Listen.] Okay — I can have that added before we even talk again. Let me do that and call you back Thursday. Fair?"
If they stall after seeing it "What would you want to change about it? I can probably fix that today."
If they say call back "No problem — I'll text you the link so you can show someone else. Talk [specific day]?"
If they're skeptical it's real "Go ahead and Google your business name right now — it should come up. [Pause.] See it?"
After a yes Collect payment, confirm their email, ask for any photos or edits. Keep it moving.

04
Common Objections — & How to Handle Them
"I get all my business through word of mouth."
"That's great — it means you're doing something right. Here's the thing though: when someone gets a recommendation for your business, the first thing they do is Google you. If they can't find you, a lot of those referrals go cold. A website doesn't replace word of mouth — it catches the ones that would otherwise slip through."
"I can't afford it / the timing isn't right."
"I completely understand. It's $100 to get started and $50 a month after that — that's less than most people spend on a business lunch. And if the site brings in even one extra customer a month, it's already paid for itself. The real question is: what's it costing you to not have one?"
"I already have a Facebook page."
"Facebook is great for staying in touch with existing customers — but it's not where people go when they're searching for a business they don't know yet. Google is. A website shows up in search results. A Facebook page mostly doesn't. They work differently, and you really need both."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. I'll leave the site up for now — it's already live. But just so you know, once I move on to the next business I'll take it down and rebuild it for them instead. So no pressure, but if you want it, this week's the window. Can I check back in with you Thursday?"
"It looks too generic / doesn't look like my business."
"Yep, that's fair — and that's honestly the point of this call. I built it from whatever was publicly available about you, so it's a skeleton right now. What you're looking at is the structure and the layout. Once you're on board, we update everything — your photos, your actual services, your wording. What you see now is the before. I'm asking if you want the after."
"I'm too busy to deal with a website."
"It's already built — that's the whole point. You don't have to do anything to get it. I just need a yes or no. The site runs itself after that. Can I text you the link and call you back in an hour when you've had a second to look?"
Remember
You're not pitching an idea — you're handing them something that already exists. The site is the proof. Get it in front of their eyes as fast as possible and let it do the talking.
